## Comparison diagram

The diagram below uses two **subgraphs** placed in a `graph LR` layout to compare two approaches side by side.

<MermaidDiagram chart={`graph LR
    subgraph ApproachA["Approach A"]
        A1[Simple Setup] --> A2[Manual Scaling]
        A2 --> A3[Lower Cost]
    end
    subgraph ApproachB["Approach B"]
        B1[Complex Setup] --> B2[Auto Scaling]
        B2 --> B3[Higher Cost]
    end
    style ApproachA fill:#ebf8ff,stroke:#3182ce
    style ApproachB fill:#fefcbf,stroke:#d69e2e
`} />

## Feature comparison

The table below compares the two approaches across several dimensions.

| Dimension     | Approach A          | Approach B            |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| Setup         | Simple, minimal     | Complex, configurable |
| Scalability   | Manual intervention | Automatic, elastic    |
| Cost          | Lower baseline      | Pay-per-use, variable |
| Typical fit   | Small workloads     | Large-scale systems   |

## When to use each

Choose the approach that matches your requirements. Approach A suits teams that value **simplicity** and predictable costs. Approach B is better when **automatic scaling** and higher throughput are critical.
